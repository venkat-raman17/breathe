# Breathe — Content Knowledge Base (Content Layer)

> **Scope & stance.** Everything below is presented as *living belief, tradition, and felt-experience* — explicitly **not** medical, scientific, or anatomical fact. Where a model is a modern Western synthesis it is labeled as such. Where sources genuinely disagree, the disagreement is shown rather than resolved. Three app-mode labels are used throughout: **Body & Breath** (mechanics/physiology of breathing), **Felt Sense** (interoceptive/somatic experience), **Tradition** (lineage-specific, belief-based practice). Safety overlays are a separate, non-tradition layer.

## 1. Master List of Traditions / Lineages

| # | Tradition / Lineage | Respectful framing (2–3 sentences) | Attribution / sensitivity note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Indian Yogic (Hindu Tantra & Hatha Yoga)** | Prana is the vital life-force of which breath is the grossest expression; pranayama regulates it through channels (nadis) and centers (chakras) toward self-realization (moksha). Codified from the Upanishads and Patanjali through the medieval Hatha corpus (Hatha Yoga Pradipika, Gheranda Samhita, Shiva Samhita). | Living Hindu/Tantric religious soteriology, not generic wellness; classically transmitted guru-to-student. The rainbow 7-chakra scheme is a modern synthesis (see §6). |
| 2 | **Yogic Subtle Body (Nadis/Chakras/Kundalini)** | A contemplative-visionary map of channels, centers, and a latent coiled energy (kundalini) that rises through the central channel to the crown. Located in the "subtle body" experienced in meditation, not in dissection. | No single canonical system; petal counts, colors, deities, and chakra number vary by text and era. Kundalini work is considered powerful and potentially destabilizing. |
| 3 | **Chinese Taoist (Daoist) Qigong / Neidan** | Qi is the vital breath/energy that is the dynamic expression of the Dao; it is gathered, circulated, and refined in the dantian and through meridians and the Du/Ren vessels. A living religious and medical tradition (Quanzhen, Zhengyi; TCM). | Daoism is a recognized world religion with clergy and lineages. Dantian are not chakras; the Microcosmic Orbit is not kundalini. Avoid New-Age flattening. |
| 4 | **Tibetan Vajrayana (tsa-lung / tummo)** | Subtle-body practices working with channels (tsa), winds (lung), and drops (tigle) to draw the winds into the central channel and realize the clear-light mind united with emptiness. The Six Yogas of Naropa are the classic frame. | Highest Yoga Tantra requiring empowerment (abhiseka), samaya vows, and a qualified guru. Sacred, initiatory, and partly self-secret — present descriptively, not as DIY. |
| 5 | **Buddhist Breath-Meditation (Anapanasati)** | Mindfulness of breathing as taught in the Anapanasati Sutta (MN 118): the breath is *observed*, not controlled, across sixteen steps and four tetrads. Foundation of both calm (samatha) and insight (vipassana). | Living Buddhist tradition; modern secular mindfulness descends from it (Burmese Vipassana, Thai Forest lineages). Theravada has no chakra/kundalini system. |
| 6 | **Japanese Hara / Ki / Zazen** | The hara (lower belly) is the body's vital center and seat of ki; zazen and budo cultivate breath and energy settled in the seika tanden / "one point." Sōtō favors natural breath; Rinzai actively cultivates the tanden. | Tanden is the Japanese reading of Chinese dantian (Daoist origin), not an indigenous invention. Non-mantric; do not map onto Indian chakras. |
| 7 | **Sufi / Islamic Contemplative** | Breath (nafas) is a sacred link to God; dhikr rides the breath, and the lataif (subtle centers) are purified to reach union. Grounded in the Qur'an and elaborated by orders (Naqshbandi, Kubrawi, Mevlevi, Inayatiyya). | Initiatic Islamic practice requiring a shaykh and silsila; the divine Names are worship, not relaxation tools. Lataif resemble chakras superficially but are a distinct cosmology. |
| 8 | **Eastern Orthodox Christian (Hesychasm)** | The Jesus Prayer is repeated until it "descends from mind into heart" and becomes self-acting, synchronized with the breath. Aims at communion with the personal God of Christian faith via katharsis, theoria, theosis. | Living ecclesial practice; breath technique is strictly secondary to humility, repentance, and a spiritual father (geron/starets). Not a stress technique. |
| 9 | **Sikh (Naam Simran / Anahad Naad)** | Loving remembrance of the divine Name (Waheguru) carried on every breath (swaas-swaas), a householder's discipline rather than forced breath-control. The "unstruck sound" is heard inwardly by grace. | A living world religion with the Guru Granth Sahib as eternal Guru. Must NOT be conflated with "Kundalini Yoga"/3HO. No chakra system. |
| 10 | **Jain (Preksha Dhyana)** | An independent dharmic tradition centered on the soul (jiva), karmic matter, and radical non-harm (ahimsa); breath is one of ten "vitalities" and the vehicle of impartial perception. Preksha Dhyana is a 20th-c. Svetambara Terapanth systematization. | Distinct from Hindu/Buddhist; prana = ten vitalities (breath is one), not a cosmic force. Chaitanya kendras are explicitly not chakras. |
| 11 | **Korean (Sundo / Seon / Muism)** | Sundo's danjeon ho-heup is slow nostril abdominal breathing; Korean Seon uses the breath as a metronome for hwadu inquiry. Shares a Sinitic Daoist/TCM ancestry via the danjeon. | Danjeon is the Korean dantian. Muism (shamanism) is possession-based, not a breath technique. Sundo's ancient-origin claims are devotional, not historically verified. |
| 12 | **Vietnamese Thiền (incl. Plum Village)** | Largely transmitted from Chinese Chan, with the native Trúc Lâm ("Bamboo Grove") school; Thích Nhất Hạnh's Plum Village conscious-breathing/gatha practice is its most global form. | Buddhist practice (ānāpānasati-derived); attribute Plum Village teachings to Thích Nhất Hạnh, not generic "wellness." |
| 13 | **Indigenous Americas (Andean, Nahua, Maya)** | Cosmological concepts of vitality, breath, and life-force (camay/sami-hucha; tonalli/teyolia/ihiyotl; ik'/ch'ulel) — relational and ceremonial, not breathing techniques. Living traditions of distinct peoples. | Several are partly closed. The Nahua "three souls" and popular Andean sami/hucha systems are scholarly/modern systematizations; "Munay-Ki" is disputed as fabricated. |
| 14 | **Australian Aboriginal (didgeridoo/yiḏaki)** | Circular-breathing drone within the Dreaming and clan Law; the continuous sound is, in some narratives, ancestral breath. Embedded in country and ceremony. | Hundreds of distinct nations, not a monolith. Some play is restricted "men's business" (regional, not universal). No general teachable "Aboriginal breath practice." |
| 15 | **Hawaiian / Pacific (hā & mana)** | Hā is the breath of life and mana is spiritual power flowing through genealogy; the honi shares breath nose-to-nose. Living Native Hawaiian/Oceanic concepts. | **"Huna" is a 20th-century New Age invention by Max Freedom Long (from 1936), NOT traditional Hawaiian** — rejected by Native Hawaiian authorities (Charles Kenn: "not, and never was Hawaiian"). Mana and hā are genuine; Huna is not. |
| 16 | **Native American (Lakota, illustrative)** | Niyá (breath/life) within an all-pervading sacred (wakȟáŋ); the inípi sweat-lodge steam is purificatory. A living, largely closed ceremonial tradition. | No pan-Indian belief; Lakota material is illustrative only. Ceremonies are sacred/closed — do not reproduce as techniques; non-Native sweat lodges have caused deaths. |
| 17 | **African & Afro-diaspora (Yoruba, Bantu, etc.)** | Àṣẹ is the power-to-make-things-happen carried in breath and voiced word; èmí is the divine breath-soul. Carried into Lukumí/Santería and Candomblé. | Initiatic living religions; deep practice belongs to initiates under elders. "Vital force/NTU" is a contested colonial-era scholarly framing. Do not stage possession. |
| 18 | **Modern Breathwork (WHM, Holotropic, Rebirthing, CCB, Transformational, Clarity, BBTRS)** | 20th–21st-c. Western practices using intensified/connected breathing for psychological and energetic transformation; framed in science/wellness or energy language. Named founders and lineages. | Mostly belief systems with real safety risks (see §6). **Rebirthing-Breathwork ≠ the coercive "rebirthing" that killed Candace Newmaker** (see §6). |
| 19 | **Modern Science-Informed Breathing (coherent/resonance, HeartMath, Buteyko, Oxygen Advantage, box, 4-7-8, physiological sigh, Papworth, diaphragmatic)** | Secular, physiology-framed techniques (autonomic balance, HRV, baroreflex, CO2 tolerance), most re-engineered from older yogic disciplines. Evidence is real but uneven. | Distinguish strong claims (slow breathing raises HRV/RSA) from weak/contested (HeartMath "coherence," 4-7-8 specifics). HeartMath/Oxygen Advantage/BOLT are branded terms. |
| 20 | **Meditation Styles (MBSR, body scan, metta, mantra/TM, Yoga Nidra, Nada, trataka, visualization, walking, open awareness)** | A family of contemplative methods differing in how they use breath, sensation, sound, and imagery; some Buddhist, some Hindu/Tantric, some modern-secular recombinations. | Attribute precisely: TM's secular framing vs. its Hindu puja is contested (Malnak v. Yogi, 1977); "McMindfulness" critique; Yoga Nidra trademarked by Bihar School. |
| 21 | **Western Esoteric (Pneuma, Vitalism, Mesmerism, Theosophy/Aura, Orgone)** | A layered Western genealogy re-theorizing "breath = life-force": Greek pneuma, vital force, animal magnetism, Theosophical subtle bodies/aura, Reich's orgone. Belief and philosophy, not validated physiology. | Mesmer's fluid was rejected (1784 commission); orgone has no scientific support. The rainbow chakra system is a Theosophical/New-Age reinterpretation of Indian sources. |
| 22 | **Ancient Near Eastern & Hebrew/Kabbalah** | Across Egypt, Mesopotamia, and the Hebrew Bible, "breath" and "life" overlapped (ka/ba; napištu; nefesh/ruaḥ/neshamah). Later Kabbalah layered a graded soul; Abulafian letter-practice pairs breath with divine-name recitation. | The graded NaRaNḤaY soul is a *medieval* mystical development, not the plain biblical sense. Name-practices are esoteric, traditionally restricted. |
| 23 | **Singing / Chanting / Recitation (cross-tradition vocal breath)** | Voiced sound (Om, Vedic chant, Quranic tajwid, Gregorian plainchant, Tibetan/Tuvan/Mongolian overtone, kirtan, gospel) is a universal exhale-lengthening technology. Sacred sound shaping the breath. | Each is a living sacred tradition with its own restrictions (tajwid accuracy; restricted Vedic mantras; initiatory Gyuto chant; contested khoomei attribution). Don't reduce to "wellness sound." |

## 6. Attribution & Labeling Guidance

**Overarching stance.** Present everything in this knowledge base as **living belief, tradition, and felt-experience — never as biomedical, scientific, or anatomical fact.** Attribute concepts to specific traditions, texts, eras, and (where relevant) named teachers/lineages, not to generic "wellness." Keep the medical-safety layer (below) visibly separate from belief content so sacred practices are not reduced to "hazards."

### 6.1 General principles
- **Frame energetic/physiological claims as the tradition's own model**, with language like "in this tradition," "practitioners describe," "is believed to."
- **Do not flatten distinct systems into one "energy."** Prana (Hindu), qi (Daoist), lung (Tibetan), ki (Japanese), ruaḥ/ruach (Hebrew), pneuma (Greek), àṣẹ (Yoruba), mana (Hawaiian), niyá (Lakota) live in different cosmologies. Dantian ≠ chakra; lataif ≠ chakra; sefirot ≠ chakra; koshas ≠ centers. Note parallels as *parallels*, not equivalences.
- **Use accurate transliteration** (e.g., prāṇa, suṣumnā, kuṇḍalinī, gtum mo, qì, danjeon, đan điền, lataif, sefirot) and acknowledge that spellings/diacritics vary by lineage and Romanization scheme.
- **Show contestation honestly** where sources disagree (chakra colors/counts, vayu correspondences, exact breath ratios, dantian location, lataif colors).

### 6.2 Mandatory corrections / non-conflation rules
- **The rainbow 7-chakra scheme is a 20th-c. Western synthesis, not classical Tantra.** Classical sources (e.g., Sat-Cakra-Nirupana, via Woodroffe's *The Serpent Power*, 1918) assign element/petal-derived colors that do **not** match the rainbow — e.g., **Manipura's petals are rain-cloud/dark-blue with an inner *red* fire-triangle (not yellow); Vishuddha has smoky-purple petals with a *white* moon-disc center (not blue).** The rainbow-plus-psychological-qualities system was crystallized by Christopher Hills (*Nuclear Evolution*, 1977), building on C.W. Leadbeater's *The Chakras* (1927, itself not the rainbow), and popularized by Anodea Judith's *Wheels of Life* (1987). Per Kurt Leland (*Rainbow Body*, 2016), rainbow colors + qualities first appeared together only in 1977. Label the rainbow palette as **modern**, not "ancient."
- **"Huna" is a modern New Age invention (Max Freedom Long, from 1936), NOT traditional Hawaiian religion** — rooted in New Thought/Theosophy and rejected by Native Hawaiian authorities (Charles Kenn: "not, and never was Hawaiian"; Pali Jae Lee; Lisa Kahaleole Hall). **Hā and mana ARE genuine.** Note: Charlotte Berney is a Huna *proponent*, not a critic.
- **Rebirthing-Breathwork (Leonard Orr) ≠ the coercive "rebirthing" that killed 10-year-old Candace Newmaker (2000).** Newmaker died of asphyxia during a physical womb-simulation restraint within unlicensed attachment therapy (practitioners convicted; "Candace's Law," 2001). Orr's practice is non-coercive connected breathing with no restraint. **Never conflate the two.**
- **"Kundalini Yoga"/3HO (Yogi Bhajan) is a syncretic modern movement, not mainstream Sikhi.** Don't present it as Sikhism; Sikhi has no chakra/kundalini system and no living guru. (Yogi Bhajan was found "more likely than not" to have committed serious abuse.)
- **Falun Gong is a modern (1992) movement; present neutrally and as contested** (banned/persecuted in PRC since 1999; teachings and media also criticized). Do not assert its metaphysical claims as fact, and don't take sides as "persecuted faith" vs. "cult." Clarify its swastika is the auspicious wan/srivatsa, unrelated to the Nazi symbol.
- **TM is marketed secular but begins with a Hindu puja; ruled religious in Malnak v. Yogi (1977).** Present the tension; don't assert "purely secular."
- **The Nahua "three souls" and popular Andean "sami/hucha" systems are scholarly/modern systematizations**, not verbatim ancient doctrine (teyolia may be partly a colonial coinage; "Munay-Ki" is disputed as fabricated).
- **The Wim Hof Method's peer-reviewed support is genuine but narrow** (a small Radboud endotoxin RCT in healthy men showing voluntary autonomic/immune modulation; 2024 review rates most WHM trials high-risk-of-bias). Do not present it as disease treatment. State the hard safety rule (never in/near water or while driving) and note death counts are *reported/alleged* compilations, not a verified registry.

### 6.3 Sacredness, initiation, and closed knowledge
- **Initiatory practices (Vajrayana tummo/tsa-lung, Sufi lataif & name-dhikr, Kabbalistic name-practice, Tantric deity yoga, Andean despacho)** require empowerment/initiation and a qualified teacher. Present **descriptively/educationally, not as DIY**, and direct serious interest to authentic teachers.
- **Closed/ceremonial traditions (Lakota inípi & other Native ceremonies; Yoruba/diaspora initiation & possession; restricted Aboriginal "men's/women's business")** must not be reproduced as techniques, staged, or commercialized. Note real harm (e.g., non-Native sweat-lodge deaths) without endorsing.
- **Sacred symbols, divine Names, and scriptures** (Allah/Hu, the Tetragrammaton, Guru Granth Sahib, Qur'an/tajwid, deity images, yantras/mandalas, the ankh) are objects of worship/heritage — never use decoratively, flippantly, or commercially. The Qur'an must be presented reverently and unaltered; Sikh Shabad Kirtan sings the living Guru and must not be casually altered.

### 6.4 Non-medical / safety labeling (separate layer)
- The app is **not a medical authority.** Present safety information as general guidance and recommend professional medical advice for anyone with listed conditions; never diagnose or treat.
- **Hard water/driving rule:** never do hyperventilation-style breathing (WHM, Holotropic, CCB, intense pranayama) or breath-holds **in or near water or while driving** — fainting/hypoxic blackout can be fatal. Always practice seated or lying down.
- **High-ventilation/breath-retention contraindications to surface on warning screens:** uncontrolled/severe hypertension & cardiovascular disease, aneurysm/cerebrovascular disease, pregnancy, epilepsy/seizure history, glaucoma/retinal detachment, recent surgery, severe asthma/COPD, panic disorder, psychosis/bipolar mania.
- **Expected-vs-stop sensations:** tingling, lightheadedness, mild tetany are commonly framed as transient; **stop** on spreading numbness, full hand-claw spasm, graying/tunnel vision, faintness, chest pain, severe headache, or panic/derealization.
- **"Spiritual emergency" framing:** intense practice can precipitate kundalini-syndrome / qigong-reaction-type states. These are partly culture-bound clinical labels (DSM "Religious or Spiritual Problem," V62.89/Z65.8); traditions often regard the same experiences as legitimate spiritual events needing guided support, not pathology. Present both views; recommend professional support for distress. Do **not** imply any tradition is inherently dangerous — documented risk attaches to **intense voluntary hyperventilation/breath-hold and to unsupervised, forced, or in-water practice.**

### 6.5 Modern vs. ancient labeling
Explicitly mark as **modern syntheses/reconstructions** (even where built on ancient vocabulary): the rainbow chakra system; Theosophical aura/subtle-body ladder; Reichian orgone; "core shamanism" & Holotropic Breathwork; Wim Hof Method (inspired-by, not equivalent to, Tibetan tummo); Preksha Dhyana's structured technique set; the popular Andean sami/hucha energy system; the Nahua tripartite-soul reconstruction; and the modern breathing-app circle/box visual grammar (inspired by, not equivalent to, sacred diagrams). Frame branded/trademarked terms (Holotropic Breathwork®, Transformational Breath®, HeartMath, Oxygen Advantage, BOLT, emWave) as attributed products, not generic tradition.
